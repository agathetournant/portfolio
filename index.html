About Me

I'm a biomedical engineer with a passion for making AI actually useful in medicine — not just impressive on paper, but genuinely impactful for patients and clinicians. My path has taken me from a Physics degree to an MSc at ETH Zurich, a research stint at MIT working on interpretable AI for computational psychiatry, and now life sciences consulting, where I've learned how AI ideas meet the messiness of real healthcare systems.

What excites me most is the space where machine learning meets neuroscience and medical imaging — understanding the brain well enough to build tools that actually help. I'm currently looking to get back closer to that science, whether through a PhD or a role where I can get my hands dirty with data that matters.

When I'm not thinking about neural data, you'll find me baking something ambitious, swimming in Lake Zurich in summer, or chasing powder on a ski slope in winter. I'm also a big reader — usually something that blurs the line between science and everything else.
